Project 2. 2-Channel, low-power, wireless EEG system PCB design & fabrication

Design Background

EEG signals have amplitude from microvolts to hundred microvolts and frequency among 0.1-50Hz when measured on the scalp. This project is to design a scalp EEG system to capture an alpha wave at the time when the human subject closes the eyes, which involves hardware design to achieve signal acquisition, filtering, amplification and signal processing.
The analog-front-end circuit has filters and amplifiers stages. The CMRR is high enough to guarantee it can acquire the clean signal from the scalp. Altium Designer is used for circuit simulation and PCB design. After ADC stage and brain-computer interfacing, MATLAB is used for post processing to make the alpha wave prominent.

Fig 2.1 2-channel scalp EEG system

Experiment Design

The full schematic of design includes two EEG channels, one shared reference channel, and one right leg drive (RLD) channel. The 1st stage acquires signals from EEG electrodes by using instrumentation amplifier. The 2nd stage passes the band between 0.1 and 50Hz. The 3rd and 4th stage are to achieve total amplification of 72.5dB using non-inverting operational amplifiers. All operational amplifiers used can operate with a single voltage supply of 3V, the VCC/2 reference voltage can be generated by a voltage divider to rise bodyâ€™s baseline potential for signal integrity. The negative feedback in RLD channel reduces common-mode voltage. And in case of leakage, when the operational amplifier saturates, the 1Mohm resistor is between the body and GND to limit current.

Results

Frequency response
The frequency response of simulation (performed in Altium Designer) has a flat passband among 0.071-53Hz with the gain of 72.4dB.
In bench testing, the passband is found by manually adjusting input signal frequency until the output amplitude decreased by 3dB (becomes 0.707 times of Vout/Vi) through filtering stage.
The passband is 0.15-64Hz.

Common-mode rejection ratio (CMRR)
The CMRR can be tested by inputting a common-mode signal with a larger amplitude to both instrumenation amplifier (INA) input pins and record the output for calculation. For example, inputting a common-mode voltage (Vcm_in) with the magnitude of 500mV to both INA input pins turns out an output of Vcm_out to be 66.8mV. And then the common-mode gain (Acm) can be obtained for CMRR calculation.
The simulated CMRR is 96dB. And the bench testing CMRR is 92.45dB.

DC offset tolerance
Electrodes may produce a DC offset voltage of hundred millivolts.
In simulation, the EEG system can tolerate 200mV DC offset by high pass filtering with 0.1Hz to attenuate low frequencies and block DC.
In bench testing, the tolerance can be found by increasing DC offset amplitude until the output signal is distorted.

Post processing
After ADC stages, the performance of this EEG system is demonstrated on a brain-computer interface P300 speller with electrodes on the subject's scalp to acquire EEG signals.
The EEG signal with significant alpha waves can be captured when the subject suddenly closes the eyes. A low-pass filter with a 10Hz cut-off frequency and a bandpass filter with 1-10Hz cut off frequency are used to post process the signal in MATLAB, which makes the alpha wave prominent.
